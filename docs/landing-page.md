## The Shift

**Before.** Forecasts get built in spreadsheets, owned by different functions, and reconciled in a weekly meeting. The number that lands in the board pack is negotiated — not computed. Your operations team plans against one version, your sales team commits to another, and your channel partners never see the consolidated view at all. When a market shift hits, you can't re-run the forecast inside a week — and there's no audit trail when leadership asks how the number was built.

**After.** One forecast surface — sliced by every dimension your business actually plans against. Each function adjusts during a defined window. The Monday meeting drops to fifteen minutes because the system did the reconciliation. The audit trail is automatic. The forecast re-runs on schedule or on demand. Your channel partners contribute their signal directly.

This isn't a system upgrade. It's a change in how your company produces the most important number it makes — and the technology follows the change, not the other way around.

## Our Approach

**Start with the metric you're on the hook to move.** Before any technology decision, we name the specific number — forecast accuracy, working capital tied up in inventory, customer-commitment delivery rate, days-to-revise-forecast. The number anchors the engagement. Everything downstream follows from it.

**Architect the forecast itself.** Dimensions and measures aren't a configuration task — they're a strategic decision about how your business sees itself. We sit with your operations, finance, and revenue teams and design the forecast that matches how you actually plan: product hierarchies, ship-from locations, territories, business units, channel partners. The technical build comes after this work, not in place of it.

**Pick the smallest change that moves the number.** Sometimes that means standing up a new forecasting system. Sometimes it means integrating signal feeds between systems you already run. Sometimes it means fixing the operating model before any system change at all. We pick the path with the largest outcome and the smallest surface area — and we tell you which one your situation actually needs.

**Implement with senior practitioners.** The people in the pitch are the people in the build. No bench, no junior handoff. The technically gnarly work that derails most engagements doesn't derail ours because we do it ourselves.

**Tie success to the business case.** The SOW closes on the outcome you committed to — forecast-accuracy delta, working-capital release, stockout reduction — not on the go-live date. If we don't move the number, the engagement isn't done.

## When This Isn't the Right Fit

We won't pitch you a result we can't deliver. This approach doesn't land if any of these apply:

- **You already run a mature, trusted demand-planning system** that your team has invested years in. Better fit: integrate the signals from your customer system into that platform. Don't duplicate the workload.
- **Your sales motion is purely transactional.** Spot sales don't generate enough relationship signal for multi-dimensional forecasting to add lift.
- **Your S&OP cadence is already mature** — monthly cross-functional meeting, named owners by function, forecast accuracy above eighty-five percent. The marginal lift over a working process doesn't justify the cost.
- **Your customer system doesn't have your customer commitments, channel-partner relationships, and sales agreements structured in it yet.** That's a foundational project, not a forecasting one — and we'd start there.
- **Your team doesn't have the analytical-engineering capability to run multi-dimensional data transformations.** That capability gap needs solving before any system goes in.
