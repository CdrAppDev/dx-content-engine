Subject: Your forecast is negotiated, not computed
Preview: And it's why your operations team can't deliver against it

Most mid-market commercial companies don't have a forecasting problem. They have a forecast-assembly problem.

The number that lands in the board pack each quarter isn't computed — it's negotiated. Four functions own four spreadsheets. The Monday meeting reconciles them. Sales says one thing, operations says another, and channel partners don't see any of it.

The downstream cost shows up everywhere: overstocked SKUs in some warehouses, stockouts in others, customer commitments your operations team can't deliver against. Forecast revisions take days because the data lives in too many places. And when leadership asks how the number was built, there's no audit trail.

A different operating model is possible — one forecast surface, every function adjusting during a role-based window, the system doing the reconciliation. Monday meetings drop to fifteen minutes. Audit trail is automatic. Channel-partner demand signal flows into the consolidated forecast for the first time.

We put a page together on how this actually works — including the cases where the math doesn't justify it. (We won't pitch you a result we can't deliver. Sometimes the gap is in the operating model, not the technology.)
