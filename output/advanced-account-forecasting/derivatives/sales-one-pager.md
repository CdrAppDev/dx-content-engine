# Stop Negotiating Your Forecast. Compute It.

**For operations leaders at mid-market commercial companies — when the forecast that lands in your board pack is negotiated, not computed.**

## The Result
One forecast surface that sales, operations, finance, and channel partners all act on — moving forecast accuracy into board-defensible territory, releasing working capital tied up in misaligned inventory, and giving you an audit trail when leadership asks how the number was built. The Monday meeting drops to fifteen minutes. Forecast revisions happen in hours.

## How We Get There
- Start with the metric you're on the hook to move (forecast accuracy, working capital, customer-commitment delivery rate)
- Architect the forecast as a strategic decision — dimensions, measures, role-based adjustment windows — before any technology decision
- Pick the smallest change that moves the number; sometimes a new system, sometimes an integration, sometimes a process fix
- Implement with senior practitioners — the people in the pitch are the people in the build
- Close the SOW on the outcome, not the go-live date

## What You Walk Away With
- One consolidated forecast grid sliced by every dimension your business actually plans against
- Role-based adjustment windows for sales, operations, finance, and channel partners
- Forecast revisions in hours, not days
- A defensible audit trail your board can read
- Working capital released as inventory positioning catches up to actual demand
- Channel-partner demand signals flowing into the consolidated forecast for the first time

## When This Isn't the Right Fit
- You already run a mature, trusted demand-planning system — better fit is integrating signals, not duplicating the workload
- Your sales motion is purely transactional — not enough relationship signal for multi-dimensional forecasting to add lift
- Your S&OP cadence is already mature (>85% accuracy, named owners by function) — marginal lift doesn't justify the cost
