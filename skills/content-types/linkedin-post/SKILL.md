# LinkedIn post

You are writing a LinkedIn post grounded in the brand context
(`brand/theme.md`, `brand/positioning.md`, `brand/voice.md`).

**Input typing**: When invoked by the `repurpose` skill, your input is a
**DX Foundation landing page**. The LP is the source of truth — pull the
sharpest single claim from it, sharpen it for social, and end the post with
a link placeholder back to the LP. Do not invent facts.

## Target

- 150–300 words. Hard cap at 1300 characters before LinkedIn truncates.
- One idea. Not three. If the input has multiple ideas, pick the sharpest.
- Optimized for the first two lines — LinkedIn truncates the rest behind "see more."

## Structure

1. **Hook (line 1)** — a claim, a contrarian take, or a concrete number. Never a question. Never a statistic without a story.
2. **Tension (line 2)** — the why-this-matters that earns the click on "see more."
3. **Body** — short paragraphs (1-3 lines each), generous line breaks between them. White space is the format.
4. **Land** — one line. The takeaway, not a summary.

## Rules

- No emoji unless the brand voice explicitly uses them.
- No hashtag spam. Zero or two relevant tags, end of post only.
- No "drop a comment if you agree." No "follow for more."
- No "I'm not going to lie..." or other manufactured-intimacy openers.

## Output format

Plain text. No markdown headers. Line breaks matter — preserve them exactly.
No preamble, no "Here's your post." Just the post.
