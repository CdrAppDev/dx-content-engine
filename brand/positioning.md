# Positioning

Who DX Foundation serves, what makes us different, what to claim.

## Who it's for

**Decision-makers at mid-market commercial companies — across every department and every vertical.**

Concretely: C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO, CRO, CIO, CMO, CHRO) and VP-level operators at companies roughly $50M–$1B in revenue. Any industry — manufacturing, distribution, professional services, financial services, healthcare, retail, etc. Any function — revenue, operations, finance, HR, IT, customer experience.

The unifying trait is **commercial mid-market**: large enough to need real software systems, small enough that one person (or a small leadership team) can decide to change them.

## What they currently believe / do

- "Software vendors and integrators all sound the same. They lead with their platform, not my problem."
- "Our last implementation went live but didn't move the metric we cared about."
- "I can't tell which features actually matter from the demo or the SOW."
- They've either skipped strategy entirely or paid for a strategy deck that didn't survive contact with the implementation.

## What we want them to believe / do after

- "DX Foundation starts with my business problem, picks the technology second, and ties every recommendation to a business outcome I can defend to my board."
- "If a feature doesn't measurably move performance, they won't sell it to me."
- "These are the people I call before I sign a software contract — not after."

## Why us, not the alternatives

- **Strategy-first**, not implementation-first. We're the only consultancy in this size range whose first deliverable is a business case, not a system design.
- **Vendor-neutral within a deep partner network.** We hold partnerships with the major platforms (Salesforce, Snowflake, Databricks, AWS, Mulesoft, Tableau) but we don't pick the platform first. The platform follows the problem.
- **Mid-market focus.** We're not chasing the Fortune 100 and we're not running on consultant-pool economics. We staff with senior practitioners on every engagement.
- **Outcomes in the SOW.** Engagements close on measurable business performance shifts, not on hours billed or features shipped.

## Off-limits

- **Don't lead with "Salesforce" or any other vendor name.** The platform is incidental to the positioning. Vendor names appear only as supporting examples or in customer-specific contexts.
- **Don't say "we implement [X]"** as the answer to "what do you do." The answer is some variant of "we help businesses perform better through software and technology."
- **Don't claim ROI numbers, customer names, or case-study specifics** that aren't in this file or in source material the user provided.
- **Don't use "transformation," "digital transformation," or "modernization"** as standalone nouns — they've been laundered into meaninglessness. Use them only with a concrete object ("transform the close cycle from 12 days to 5").
- **Don't pitch features.** Pitch the business shift the feature enables.
