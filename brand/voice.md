# Voice

How DX Foundation sentences sound. Loaded into every content skill.

## Voice attributes

- **Confident** — we make claims, then back them. No hedging modifiers ("might," "could potentially," "may help to").
- **Business-first** — every sentence answers "so what does this change for the business?" before "what is the thing?"
- **Plain** — sixth-to-eighth grade reading level. A CFO should be able to paraphrase any paragraph after one read.
- **Lightly warm** — confident isn't the same as cold. We use first-person plural ("we"), address the reader as "you," and the occasional one-line aside is welcome.
- **Direct** — short sentences. Verbs over adjectives. No throat-clearing.

## Casing

- **Title Case** for headlines, section titles, and marketing CTAs ("Our Comprehensive Array of Services," "Ready to Transform Your Business?")
- **Sentence case** for body, UI labels, and email subjects
- **ALL CAPS with wide tracking (0.14em)** for eyebrows / category labels — sparingly

## Person and address

- **Second person ("you," "your")** when speaking to the customer
- **First-person plural ("we," "our")** when speaking as DX Foundation
- Never first-person singular. There is no "I" in the brand voice.

## Do

- Lead with the business outcome, then explain the mechanism
- Use em dashes to chain consequences — they're a brand vocabulary marker
- Quantify where you can: "two days faster," "a $4M working-capital release," "40% fewer escalations"
- Name the buyer's department or role when it sharpens the message ("if you're the CFO," "for the head of sales operations")
- Use contractions in body copy

## Don't

- Don't write "leverage," "synergy," "robust," "seamless," "best-in-class," "next-generation," "in the realm of," "it's worth noting," "in today's fast-paced world," "world-class," "cutting-edge"
- Don't write "transformation" or "digital transformation" as a standalone noun. Use only with a concrete object.
- Don't open with a question unless it lands a real tension ("Ever hired a vendor because you loved their A-team — only to be handed off the day the contract was signed?")
- Don't close with "in conclusion," "in summary," or "to wrap up"
- Don't use exclamation points in body copy. Reserved for warm CTAs only ("Let's make it happen together!").
- Don't use emoji
- Don't lead with a platform or product name. Lead with the buyer's situation.
- **Don't pitch the technology — pitch the business outcome.** Vendor product names (Salesforce, Manufacturing Cloud, Advanced Account Forecasting, Snowflake Unistore, AWS Bedrock, etc.) belong in the SEO/AEO metadata layer, not in body copy. Default to **zero platform mentions** in the visible LP/blog/email body. Use generic functional language instead: "your customer system," "a forecasting system," "the planning platform you already run." DXF sells strategic solutions to business problems — the technology is the means, not the message. This is the rule the user enforces most strictly.

## Punctuation and quirks

- **Em dashes** chain related clauses — used liberally, replacing semicolons
- **Ampersands** are acceptable in headings ("Data, AI & Agentic Systems")
- The unicode **✦** (U+2726) is part of the brand vocabulary — usable as a divider in marquee or transitional copy
- No other unicode glyphs

## Sentence rhythm

Pattern: short, medium, long. Vary clause length deliberately. A wall of medium-length sentences reads as flat.

### Reference samples (the voice we're targeting)

```
You've spent the last two budget cycles buying technology to fix problems
nobody had clearly defined. The platforms work. The results haven't moved.

We start the other way. First we get specific about the business metric you
need to shift — close cycle, customer acquisition cost, time-to-quote,
escalation rate, whatever your board is watching. Then we pick the
technology that actually moves it, with the smallest possible surface area.
Then we implement it ourselves, with senior people on every seat.

You stop buying capabilities. You start buying outcomes — and you can name
the outcome before the contract is signed.
```

```
Plagued by data silos? You're missing opportunities for intelligent
decisions and innovation. We unify disparate sources, implement governed AI
foundations, and create scalable pipelines — so you gain real-time
insights, make confident decisions, and drive growth at scale.
```

```
Ever hired a vendor because you loved their "A team," only to be
disappointed when the project is immediately handed off? We staff every
engagement with senior practitioners — the people in the room during the
pitch are the people doing the work.
```
